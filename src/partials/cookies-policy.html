For example, we may use cookies and web beacons when user is interacting with our website, advertising, applications or using other digital resources.

A cookie is a text entry that a web server places in the client device's persistent memory. Typically, cookies are used to support authorized sessions or to configure a custom interface, font size, color scheme, site language, etc., to preserve your preferences and generally provide a more comfortable usage of the site. A web beacon, also called an Internet tag, a pixel tag, or a blank GIF file, is used to return information to a web server.

Visiting and using this site is completely anonymous. We collect mostly statistics on popular pages, site database searches, bug information and other impersonal data to further improve our services. We may also use cookies to collect information, which may include browser type, operating system, IP addresses, link source of URL, and information about actions taken with our digital resources.

Please note that the main purpose of using Cookies on this site is not to identify you as a user, but to provide a better service.

Every time you visit our Site, you will be prompted to accept or refuse cookies. The purpose is to enable the site to remember your preferences (such as username and password, language, etc.) for a certain period. That way, you don’t have to re-enter them when browsing around the site during the same visit.

There are some cookies that we must include for certain web pages to function. For this reason, they do not require your consent. In particular: authentication cookies and technical cookies (required by certain IT systems). Authentication cookies are stored when you log in to the Site. When you do this, you accept the associated privacy policy.

Cookies can also be used to establish anonymized statistics about the browsing experience on our sites. We may also use third-party web analytics services on our websites and other digital resources. Therefore, in addition to its own cookies, this site may store other additional files due to the use of third-party services, such as Google Analytics and others.

For more detailed control over site-specific cookies, check the privacy and cookie settings in your preferred browser.
